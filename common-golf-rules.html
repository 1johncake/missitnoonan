Wedghoods Local Rules

House Rules

Agree before you tee off. These apply to casual Wedghoods rounds unless the group decides otherwise.

Lift, Clean & Place
Roll it in the fairway. Down in the rough. Standard play through roughly June — Seattle mudballs are not golf shots.
Out of Bounds / Lost Ball
One-stroke penalty, drop where it crossed or was lost. Exception: if a provisional was hit, stroke and distance applies — play the provisional.
Gimmes
Inside the leather — agree with your playing partners. No unilateral gimmes in match play or when money's on the line.
Pace of Play
Ready golf always. If you're looking for a ball and holding up the group, drop and move. We're here for the round, not the search.

Players should agree on which local rules are in effect before the round. When in doubt, play it down and settle up at the 19th.

The Rules
Red Penalty Area
Red stakes or lines — lateral relief available
+
What it means
Your ball is in or touching a red-staked area, or last crossed the red boundary. One penalty stroke. You have three options:
  • 1Stroke and distance — replay from where you last hit.
  • 2Back-on-the-line relief — drop behind the penalty area on the line from the hole through where the ball crossed, going back as far as you like.
  • 3Lateral relief (red only) — drop within two club-lengths of where the ball crossed the edge, no closer to the hole. Also permitted on the opposite side of the penalty area, equidistant from the hole.
USGA Rule 17 — Penalty Areas
Yellow Penalty Area
Yellow stakes or lines — no lateral relief
+
What it means
Same as red, but no lateral relief option. One penalty stroke, two choices:
  • 1Stroke and distance — replay from where you last hit.
  • 2Back-on-the-line relief — drop on a line from the hole through where the ball crossed, going back as far as you want.
Yellow areas are typically water hazards directly in front of you. The lack of lateral relief is intentional — you're supposed to carry it.
USGA Rule 17 — Penalty Areas
Ground Under Repair
GUR — marked by white lines or signs
+
What it means
Free relief — no penalty stroke. Your ball is in a GUR area (white lines, typically). You must take relief if the area interferes with your stance or swing, even if you'd rather play it.
  • 1Find the nearest point of complete relief — the closest spot not in the GUR, not nearer the hole, where there's no interference.
  • 2Drop within one club-length of that point, no closer to the hole.
If your ball is lost in a GUR, you may drop in the GUR relief area without penalty — you don't have to take stroke and distance.
USGA Rule 16 — Abnormal Course Conditions
Casual Water / Temporary Water
Standing water not in a penalty area — free relief
+
What it means
Any temporary accumulation of water visible before or after you take your stance — no penalty stroke. Classic Seattle situation. Also covers snow and ice at your discretion.
  • 1Find the nearest point of complete relief from the water — not nearer the hole.
  • 2Drop within one club-length of that point, no closer to the hole.
This also applies if your ball is in the rough and your stance would be in casual water — you get relief for your stance, not just your ball.
USGA Rule 16.1 — Abnormal Course Conditions
Cart Path
Immovable obstruction — free relief
+
What it means
Ball on the cart path, or path interferes with your stance or swing. Free relief, no penalty.
  • 1Find the nearest point of complete relief — where the path no longer interferes with your stance, swing, or ball, not nearer the hole.
  • 2Drop within one club-length of that point, no closer to the hole.
You must take the nearest point of relief — even if that's in the rough and you'd rather be on the other side in the fairway. Nearest is nearest.
USGA Rule 16.1 — Immovable Obstructions
Immovable Obstruction
Sprinkler heads, signs, fences — free relief
+
What it means
Anything artificial and fixed that you can't move — sprinkler heads, distance markers, drainage covers, stake wires, yardage signs. Free relief if it interferes with your ball, stance, or swing.
  • 1Nearest point of complete relief, not nearer the hole.
  • 2Drop within one club-length of that point, no closer to the hole.
Note: line-of-sight interference doesn't count. The obstruction must physically interfere with your stance or swing — not just block your view of the pin.
USGA Rule 16.1 — Abnormal Course Conditions
Out of Bounds
White stakes — stroke and distance (or local rule)
+
By the book
Stroke and distance. One penalty stroke, re-hit from the original spot. Your next shot is your third.
Wedghoods local rule
One stroke penalty, drop where it crossed. Unless a provisional was hit — in that case, play the provisional under stroke and distance.
A ball is OB when all of it lies outside the white stakes. If any part touches the line or is inside, it's in bounds.
USGA Rule 18 — Out of Bounds / Lost Ball
Lost Ball
3 minutes to search — then it's gone
+
What it means
You have 3 minutes to search. After that, the ball is officially lost. Same penalty as OB — stroke and distance by the book, or the Wedghoods local rule (one stroke, drop where it was lost).
The 3-minute clock starts when you and your group reach the area and begin searching — not when you hit the shot. If you find a ball but can't identify it as yours, it's lost.
USGA Rule 18 — Out of Bounds / Lost Ball
Provisional Ball
Hit one to save time — announce it first
+
The procedure
Think your ball might be OB or lost? Before leaving the tee or hitting area, announce "I'm hitting a provisional" — you must say the word provisional. Then hit. If the original is found in bounds, play it and pick up the provisional. If not, play the provisional under stroke and distance.
You cannot hit a provisional if you think your ball is in a penalty area — that's a different procedure. Provisional is only for OB or lost in the general area.
USGA Rule 18.3 — Provisional Ball
Unplayable Lie
One stroke penalty — three options
+
What it means
Only you can declare your ball unplayable — anywhere on the course except a penalty area. One penalty stroke. Three options:
  • 1Stroke and distance — go back to where you last hit.
  • 2Back-on-the-line relief — drop on a line from the hole through the ball, going back as far as you want.
  • 3Lateral relief — drop within two club-lengths of the ball, no closer to the hole.
In a bunker, options 2 and 3 require you to drop in the bunker. Option 1 (stroke and distance) gets you out of the bunker — same one-stroke penalty. There's also a two-stroke option to drop outside the bunker on the back line.
USGA Rule 19 — Unplayable Ball
Embedded Ball (Plugged Lie)
Ball in its own pitch mark — free relief in general area
+
What it means
Your ball is plugged in its own pitch mark in the general area (fairway, rough — not a bunker or penalty area). Free relief, no penalty. Common after rain.
  • 1Mark the ball's position, lift and clean it.
  • 2Drop within one club-length of the spot directly behind where it was embedded, no closer to the hole.
No free relief for embedded balls in bunkers — play it as it lies or declare unplayable. Also, the ball must be in its own pitch mark — not just sitting down in soft ground.
